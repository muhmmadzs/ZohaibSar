Muhammad Zohaib Sarwar, PhD

I am a data scientist and engineer with a PhD from the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), working on machine learning solutions for sensor-driven engineering systems. My work spans vibration analysis, time-series modeling, anomaly detection, condition monitoring, and predictive maintenance, with a focus on building robust end-to-end workflows from data extraction and signal processing to model validation and stakeholder delivery.

I currently work as an Industrial Postdoctoral Fellow at NTNU and Norske Tog, where I develop data-driven models for railway condition monitoring and maintenance decision support. I also work with industrial partners including Resonant Engineering, ABB, and Stadler Rail on applied machine learning, monitoring pipelines, and engineering analytics.

Research

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, signal processing, and engineering systems. I work on predictive modeling, anomaly detection, uncertainty-aware learning, and condition monitoring using time-series and sensor data. My recent work includes vibration analytics for industrial assets, predictive maintenance for railway systems, and deep learning methods for structural health monitoring.

Projects
