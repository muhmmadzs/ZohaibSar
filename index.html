Muhammad Zohaib Sarwar

About

I am a computational engineer and data scientist specializing in predictive modeling and AI-driven solutions for infrastructure and transportation systems. Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), I leverage expertise in structural dynamics and machine learning to develop intelligent monitoring and predictive maintenance systems for critical infrastructure. My work integrates advanced data analytics, time-series modeling, and cloud-based IoT platforms to enable real-time condition assessment of bridges, rail networks, and other complex engineering systems. Passionate about bridging the gap between traditional engineering and modern data science, I continually seek innovative ways to enhance safety, efficiency, and sustainability in transportation and civil infrastructure.

Research

I'm interested in structural health monitoring, AI-based damage detection, and railway condition monitoring. My research focuses on developing AI-driven models for predictive maintenance and infrastructure health monitoring, bridging civil engineering and machine learning. For example, I have worked on projects such as predictive maintenance for railway networks, intelligent concrete curing systems, and autonomous UAV-based bridge inspection.

Projects

  • Predictive Maintenance for Railways (2023–Present): Developed a CI/CD workflow to deploy machine learning-based wear prediction models for railway wheels (Python, Azure, GitHub Actions).
  • Intelligent Concrete Drying System (2022–2023): Designed a sensor fusion model combining material physics and machine learning for optimized concrete curing (IoT sensors, ML, Cloud analytics).
  • Autonomous UAV for Bridge Inspection (2018–2019): Built an autonomous drone-based inspection platform using computer vision for automated defect detection in bridges.
